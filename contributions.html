Overview

Understanding how salaries are computed in the Philippines is essential for both employees and employers. Your salary undergoes several deductions before you receive your net pay. The key factors affecting your net salary include:

  • Mandatory government deductions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and Income Tax)
  • Employment benefits and allowances
  • Bonuses such as 13th-month pay and holiday pay

Mandatory Deductions

  • SSS (Social Security System) - Provides retirement, disability, and sickness benefits.
  • PhilHealth - Health insurance covering medical expenses.
  • Pag-IBIG Fund - A savings and loan program mainly for housing benefits.
  • Income Tax (BIR) - Progressive tax rates based on salary brackets.

Updated Contribution Rates for 2025

  • SSS: 15% contribution rate (Employer: 10%, Employee: 5%).
  • PhilHealth: Premium rate remains at 5%, with a minimum of ₱500 and a maximum of ₱2,500.
  • Pag-IBIG: Employee contribution is 1% (for salaries ≤₱1,500) or 2% (for salaries >₱1,500). Employer share remains 2%.
  • Income Tax: New rates from 2023 onward under the TRAIN Law:
    • ₱250,000 and below: 0% tax
    • ₱250,000 - ₱400,000: 15% of the excess over ₱250,000
    • ₱400,000 - ₱800,000: ₱22,500 + 20% of the excess over ₱400,000
    • ₱800,000 - ₱2,000,000: ₱102,500 + 25% of the excess over ₱800,000
    • ₱2,000,000 - ₱8,000,000: ₱402,500 + 30% of the excess over ₱2,000,000
    • ₱8,000,000 and above: ₱2,202,500 + 35% of the excess over ₱8,000,000

Bonuses & Benefits

  • 13th-Month Pay: Equivalent to one month's basic salary, given before December 24.
  • Overtime Pay: Additional pay for work beyond 8 hours a day.
  • Night Differential: Extra pay (10% of hourly rate) for work between 10 PM and 6 AM.
  • Holiday Pay: Employees working on regular holidays receive 200% of their daily rate.

FAQs

1. How is my take-home pay calculated?

Your take-home pay is your gross salary minus all mandatory deductions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG, and Income Tax).

2. Do contractual employees also have deductions?

Yes, as long as they are under an employer who provides government-mandated benefits.

3. What is the impact of the TRAIN Law on my salary?

Under the **TRAIN Law**, individuals earning ₱250,000 annually or below are exempt from income tax.

4. Can I increase my Pag-IBIG contribution?

Yes, voluntary contributions beyond the required 1-2% are allowed for higher savings and loan eligibility.
